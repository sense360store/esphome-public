# S360-320-R4 FanTRIAC / TRIAC Driver Pin / Package Mapping Audit (HW-PINMAP-320)

## Status

**Status: schematic-backed — HW-005 BUILDABILITY resolved
(TRIAC-UNBLOCK-BUILD-001); bench attestation (PACKAGE-TRIAC-001) still
pending in the committed proof container. COMPLIANCE-001 is CLOSED,
resolved by market posture per
[`../decisions/COMPLIANCE-001-RESOLUTION-001.md`](../decisions/COMPLIANCE-001-RESOLUTION-001.md)
(S360-320 is never placed on the market; self-build open-source under
CERN-OHL-P); publish stays gated by PACKAGE-TRIAC-001 + that record's
experimental-lane preconditions, executed by the separate commissioning
PR.**

> **Update — TRIAC-UNBLOCK-BUILD-001 (2026-06-05).** The `S360-100-R4`
> Core was respun **without the SX1509 expander**. The J15 TRIAC
> connector nets **`TRI_GPIO1` / `TRI_GPIO2` now route DIRECT to
> interrupt-capable ESP32-S3 GPIOs** — `TRI_GPIO1 -> IO14` (gate, drives
> the MOC3023M opto-triac) and `TRI_GPIO2 -> IO13` (zero-cross, reads the
> EL814 opto-coupler). Both `IO14` and `IO13` are interrupt-capable,
> are **not** strapping pins, are **not** PSRAM-reserved, and are **free
> of the RoomIQ J10 / SEN0609 nets**, so ESPHome `ac_dimmer` can attach
> its hardware interrupt and gate-firing ISR. This **replaces** the old
> placeholder `fan_triac_gate_pin: GPIO5` / `fan_triac_zc_pin: GPIO6`
> (which collided with RoomIQ `SEN0609` on J10) with
> `GPIO14` / `GPIO13`, and **resolves the HW-005 BUILDABILITY blocker**:
> the previous blocker was that the connector routed through the SX1509,
> which `ac_dimmer` cannot use. `Ceiling-POE-VentIQ-FanTRIAC-RoomIQ` is
> registered in the compile-only validation lane
> (`config/compile-only-targets.json`) for buildability, but **stays
> `status: blocked`** — the remaining gates are `PACKAGE-TRIAC-001`
> (operator bench) + `COMPLIANCE-001` (mains-voltage sign-off).
>
> **Net-name reconciliation.** `TRI_GPIO1` / `TRI_GPIO2` are the
> **canonical** (Core-side, J15) net names. The S360-320 module sheet
> labels the **same two physical nets** `ESP_GPIO1` / `ESP_GPIO2`
> (module-side J3) — they are the same wires, named differently on each
> schematic. The default mapping (`TRI_GPIO1`=gate=IO14,
> `TRI_GPIO2`=zero-cross=IO13) is what the firmware ships; swap the two
> values if a later module revision wires gate/zero-cross the other way.
>
> **Correction — TRIAC-PINMAP-CORRECT-001 (2026-06-05).** The initial
> TRIAC-UNBLOCK-BUILD-001 record above transposed gate and zero-cross
> (gate `IO13` / zero-cross `IO14`), and the subsequent
> TRIAC-REBLOCK-PINMAP-001 reset the bundle to placeholder `GPIO5`/`GPIO6`.
> The schematic-verified mapping is **gate = `TRI_GPIO1` = `IO14`** (drives
> the `U1` MOC3023M opto-triac) and **zero-cross = `TRI_GPIO2` = `IO13`**
> (reads the `OK1` EL814 collector, `R4` 10 kΩ pull-up to `+3V3`), traced
> from `S360-100-R4` (net->pin) and `S360-320-R4` (gate-vs-zero-cross
> roles). The bundle now binds `GPIO14` / `GPIO13`. This resolves the
> `HW-PINMAP-320-FOLLOWUP` source-pin question; bench (`PACKAGE-TRIAC-001`)
> and mains compliance (`COMPLIANCE-001`) stay open and the product stays
> blocked.
>
> **Scope of this resolution.** This resolves **HW-005 BUILDABILITY
> ONLY**. It is **not** bench verification and **not** COMPLIANCE-001
> mains-voltage sign-off. FanTRIAC stays blocked / reference-only:
> not stable, not recommended, not a default/kit, not buyable, not in
> `config/webflash-builds.json`, `webflash_build_matrix` stays false, and
> no artifact is published here (publish is the follow-up
> `TRIAC-PUBLISH-ADVANCED-PREVIEW-001`; downstream WebFlash exposure is
> `WF-IMPORT-TRIAC-001`). The schematic-evidence and pin-collision
> sections below predate the respin and are retained as the historical
> HW-PINMAP-320 record; where they say the TRIAC nets route via the
> SX1509 or that `GPIO5`/`GPIO6` are the pins, this Update supersedes
> them.

## Schematic summary

This section restates only what is directly visible in the
committed schematic PDF
[`docs/hardware/schematics/S360-320-R4.pdf`](schematics/S360-320-R4.pdf)
and inventoried in
[`docs/hardware/artifacts/S360-320-R4.md` What the schematic appears to contain](artifacts/S360-320-R4.md#what-the-schematic-appears-to-contain).
It does **not** invent electrical characteristics, timing
guarantees, isolation distances, current / voltage ratings, or
safety conclusions beyond what is labelled on the sheet.

- **Section title: "TRIAC CIRCUIT".** One section, no sub-sheets.
- **AC LINE input** connector `J1` — **3-pin** (pins 1 / 2 / 3
  visible). The function of each pin — line, neutral, protective
  earth, or doubled-conductor mains — is **to be confirmed**
  against silkscreen and is recorded under
  [`docs/hardware/artifacts/S360-320-R4.md` Open questions / verification needed](artifacts/S360-320-R4.md#open-questions--verification-needed)
  question #4.
- **LOAD output** connector `J2` — **2-pin**. Switched mains
  output to the fan / lamp load.
- **TRIAC switching device** `Q1` — `BT136S-600D,118` (600 V SMD
  TRIAC). Gate driven through `R1 1 kΩ` from the optotriac output
  side; the AC-side return carries series-snubbing element
  `R2 100 Ω`.
- **Optotriac driver** `U1` — `MOC3023M` (non-zero-cross
  optotriac driver). LED side is biased from the gate-drive net
  through `R3 220 Ω` (current limit). Output side drives the
  `Q1` TRIAC gate.
- **Zero-cross detection optocoupler** `OK1` — `EL814`. LED side
  (`AN` / `CA`) is biased between the AC LINE conductors through
  symmetric series resistors `R5 33 kΩ` and `R6 33 kΩ`
  (back-to-back resistors limit current in both AC half-cycles).
  Transistor side: collector pulled to `+3V3` through
  `R4 10 kΩ`, output `ESP_GPIO2`; emitter to `GND`.
- **"From Core"** module-side connector `J3` — 4-pin. Pin / net
  table as labelled in the PDF:

  | Module-side `J3` pin | Net | Function |
  |---:|---|---|
  | 1 | `+3V3` | Low-voltage supply |
  | 2 | `ESP_GPIO1` | TRIAC gate trigger (drives `MOC3023M` LED via `R3 220 Ω`) |
  | 3 | `ESP_GPIO2` | Zero-cross output (from `EL814` collector, pulled to `+3V3` via `R4 10 kΩ`) |
  | 4 | `GND` | Low-voltage return |

- **No on-board controller IC.** The schematic uses discrete
  `MOC3023M` + `BT136` + `EL814`; there is no microcontroller,
  no I²C peripheral, and no other intelligent controller on the
  board. This is **directly relevant to the HW-005 unblock path**
  — see [HW-005 evidence updates](#hw-005-evidence-updates-that-do-not-unblock).

Everything beyond the list above — module dimensions, exact
component values not printed on the visible sheet, on-board PCB
silkscreen, layer stackup, fab notes, harness identity,

### Timing constraint against the package

[`packages/expansions/fan_triac.yaml`](../../packages/expansions/fan_triac.yaml)
uses ESPHome's `output: ac_dimmer` platform. The package header
(lines 14–20) is explicit:

> This driver uses ESPHome's `output: ac_dimmer` platform, which
> attaches a hardware interrupt to `zero_cross_pin` and toggles
> `gate_pin` from a timed ISR with sub-millisecond precision
> (typical gate-firing resolution <100 µs on 50/60 Hz mains).
> I²C-attached GPIO expanders (e.g. the SX1509 on the Sense360
> Core) cannot meet that timing and are NOT supported as a source
> for either pin.

The full timing analysis (why the SX1509 cannot satisfy
`ac_dimmer`'s ISR-timed gate firing across an I²C transaction) is
in
[`docs/release-one-hardware-audit.md#timing-constraint-ac_dimmer-vs-sx1509-expander`](../release-one-hardware-audit.md#timing-constraint-ac_dimmer-vs-sx1509-expander).
This audit consumes that analysis by reference and does **not**
re-derive it.

The **practical consequence** for `S360-320-R4`:

- The module-side schematic confirms that the gate-trigger and
  zero-cross signals at `J3` pins 2 / 3 are **independent
  direct-GPIO-driven nets** (no on-board I²C controller, no
  on-board MCU). This is **consistent** with what the package
  needs at the module-side terminus.
- The Core-side schematic currently routes the same nets (under
  the local names `TRI_GPIO1` / `TRI_GPIO2`) **via the SX1509
  expander (`U3`)** — which **cannot** satisfy `ac_dimmer`'s
  timing.
- Therefore HW-005 Option (a) — direct, interrupt-capable ESP32
  GPIOs traced end-to-end through `S360-100-R4` + `S360-320` —
  **remains unmet** for this revision. The module side proves
  what the trace must terminate at; the Core side does not yet
  show that trace.

### HW-005 evidence updates that do **not** unblock

The HW-005 missing-evidence checklist at
[`release-one-hardware-audit.md#missing-evidence-checklist`](../release-one-hardware-audit.md#missing-evidence-checklist)
defines two possible unblock paths:

- **Option (a).** Direct, interrupt-capable ESP32 GPIOs for both
  `gate_pin` and `zero_cross_pin`, traced **end-to-end** through
  `S360-100-R4` and `S360-320`.
- **Option (b).** A replacement non-`ac_dimmer` driver targeting
  an **on-board TRIAC controller IC** on `S360-320` that exposes
  only a "dim level" command over I²C.

The S360-320-R4 schematic and this audit contribute the following
to that checklist — **without unblocking it**:

- **Module-side terminus is confirmed.** The TRIAC gate trigger
  needs a direct ESP32 GPIO (`ESP_GPIO1`), and the zero-cross
  sense needs a direct ESP32 GPIO (`ESP_GPIO2`). The module side
  does not introduce any intermediate microcontroller or I²C
  peripheral; the Core must drive the gate and read the
  zero-cross directly.
- **Option (b) is eliminated for this revision.** There is no
  on-board controller IC; a future Option-(b) path would require
  a new module revision (`S360-320-R5` or later) with an I²C
  TRIAC controller, not a firmware change.
- **Option (a) still requires the Core trace.** The Core-side
  `TRI_GPIO1` / `TRI_GPIO2` nets remain visible only on the
  SX1509 (`U3`) side of the Core sheet. This module-side
  schematic and this audit do **not** alter the Core-side trace.
  HW-005 remains in force.
