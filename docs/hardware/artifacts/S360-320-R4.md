### What the schematic appears to contain

Observed directly from the uploaded PDF. Every claim below is sourced
from a visible label or symbol on the single schematic sheet.

- **Section title: "TRIAC CIRCUIT".** One section, no sub-sheets.
- **AC LINE input** connector `J1` — **3-pin** (visible pins 1 / 2 /
  3; the function of each pin — line, neutral, protective earth, or
  doubled-conductor mains — is to be confirmed against silkscreen and
  is recorded under [Open questions](#open-questions--verification-needed)).
- **LOAD output** connector `J2` — 2-pin.
- **TRIAC switching device** `Q1` — `BT136S-600D,118` (600 V SMD
  TRIAC).
- **Optotriac driver** `U1` — `MOC3023M` (non-zero-cross optotriac
  driver). LED side is biased from the gate-drive net through `R3 220 Ω`
  (current limit). Output side drives the `Q1` TRIAC gate through
  `R1 1 kΩ`; series snubbing element `R2 100 Ω` on the AC-side return.
- **Zero-cross detection optocoupler** `OK1` — `EL814`. LED side
  (`AN`/`CA`) is biased between AC LINE conductors through symmetric
  series resistors `R5 33 kΩ` and `R6 33 kΩ` (back-to-back resistors
  limit current in both AC half-cycles). Transistor side: collector
  pulled to `+3V3` through `R4 10 kΩ`, output `ESP_GPIO2`; emitter to
  `GND`.
- **"From Core"** connector `J3` — 4-pin. Net list as labelled in the
  PDF:

  | Module J3 pin | Net | Function |
  |---:|---|---|
  | 1 | `+3V3` | Low-voltage supply |
  | 2 | `ESP_GPIO1` | TRIAC gate trigger (drives `MOC3023M` LED via `R3 220 Ω`) |
  | 3 | `ESP_GPIO2` | Zero-cross output (from `EL814` collector, pulled to `+3V3` via `R4 10 kΩ`) |
  | 4 | `GND` | Low-voltage return |

- **No on-board I²C TRIAC controller IC.** The schematic uses
  discrete `MOC3023M` + `BT136` + `EL814`; there is no microcontroller,
  no I²C peripheral, and no other intelligent controller on the board.
  This is **directly relevant to the HW-005 unblock path** — see
  [HW-005 evidence updates](#hw-005-evidence-updates-that-do-not-unblock).

### Reconciliation flags raised or strengthened by this schematic

1. **`TRI_GPIO*` (Core) vs `ESP_GPIO*` (Module) net-name divergence
   (new flag).** [`s360-100-r4-core.md` §J15](../s360-100-r4-core.md#j15--triac-fan-module-connector-4-pin)
   captures the Core-side J15 4-pin signals as `+3.3V`, `TRI_GPIO1`,
   `TRI_GPIO2`, `GND`. The module-side `J3` labels the same physical
   pins as `+3V3`, `ESP_GPIO1`, `ESP_GPIO2`, `GND`. The signals are
   the same wires; only the net name differs. Belongs to
   `HW-PINMAP-320`.
2. **Module-side expectation: direct ESP32 GPIOs.** The module-side
   net naming `ESP_GPIO1` / `ESP_GPIO2` is consistent with the
   expectation in
   [`packages/expansions/fan_triac.yaml`](../../../packages/expansions/fan_triac.yaml)
   that both `gate_pin` and `zero_cross_pin` are direct,
   interrupt-capable ESP32 GPIOs (per the timing analysis in
   [`release-one-hardware-audit.md#timing-constraint-ac_dimmer-vs-sx1509-expander` (archived)](../../archive-index.md)).
3. **No on-board I²C TRIAC controller IC.** The board uses discrete
   `MOC3023M` + `BT136` + `EL814`. There is no I²C peripheral, no
   on-board MCU, and no other intelligent controller on the board.
   This **eliminates Option (b)** from the HW-005 missing-evidence
   checklist (a replacement non-`ac_dimmer` driver targeting an
   on-board controller IC over I²C) — that option assumes hardware
   that does not exist on this revision.
4. **AC LINE pin count.** The AC LINE input `J1` is **3-pin**. Whether
   the third pin is protective earth (`PE`), neutral (`N`), or a
   doubled-line conductor is not derivable from the PDF alone and is
   a COMPLIANCE-001-adjacent question — see
   [Open questions](#open-questions--verification-needed) #4.

### HW-005 evidence updates that **do not** unblock

The HW-005 missing-evidence checklist at
[`release-one-hardware-audit.md#missing-evidence-checklist` (archived)](../../archive-index.md)
defines two possible unblock paths:

- **Option (a).** Direct, interrupt-capable ESP32 GPIOs for both
  `gate_pin` and `zero_cross_pin`, traced **end-to-end** through
  `S360-100-R4` and `S360-320`.
- **Option (b).** A replacement non-`ac_dimmer` driver targeting an
  **on-board TRIAC controller IC** on `S360-320` that exposes only a
  "dim level" command over I²C.

The S360-320-R4 schematic delivered by this PR contributes the
following to that checklist — **without unblocking it**:

- **Module-side terminus is confirmed.** The TRIAC gate trigger needs
  a direct ESP32 GPIO (`ESP_GPIO1`), and the zero-cross sense needs a
  direct ESP32 GPIO (`ESP_GPIO2`). The module-side does not introduce
  any intermediate microcontroller or I²C peripheral; the Core must
  drive the gate and read the zero-cross directly.
- **Option (b) is eliminated.** There is no on-board controller IC on
  this revision; a future Option-(b) path would require a new module
  revision (`S360-320-R5` or later) with an I²C TRIAC controller, not
  a firmware change.
- **Option (a) still requires the Core trace.** The Core-side `J15`
  capture in
  [`s360-100-r4-core.md` §J15](../s360-100-r4-core.md#j15--triac-fan-module-connector-4-pin)
  shows `TRI_GPIO1` / `TRI_GPIO2` appearing only on the SX1509 (`U3`)
  side of the Core sheet, and the SX1509 cannot satisfy
  `ac_dimmer`'s timing requirements. **This module-side schematic
  does not change the Core-side trace** and does not, on its own,
  satisfy the end-to-end direct-ESP32-GPIO requirement. The HW-005
  blocker remains in force until either:
  - the Core schematic is re-traced and a direct ESP32 GPIO route to
    `J15` pins 2 / 3 is documented (preferred path), or
  - a Core revision is taped out that routes direct ESP32 GPIOs to
    `J15` pins 2 / 3 (hardware respin path).

### COMPLIANCE-001 (mains-voltage UK / EU) — unchanged

**COMPLIANCE-001 status: in force, no change.** This board operates
on mains AC voltage. Before any FanTRIAC-bearing config can ship —
even after HW-005 is independently resolved — an electrical safety
and compliance review per
[`docs/compliance/mains-voltage-uk-eu-assessment.md`](../../compliance/mains-voltage-uk-eu-assessment.md)
must clear. That review must address at minimum: isolation barriers
between mains and low-voltage logic; creepage and clearance distances
on the PCB (which require Gerbers / KiCad PCB source, neither of which
was uploaded); fusing and over-current protection; PCB finger and
connector ratings; CE / UKCA / FCC / UL applicability for the
intended deployment region; appropriate enclosure and ingress
protection; and any standards specific to TRIAC phase-cut dimming of
fans and lamps.

**Documentation alone is not sufficient to ship mains hardware.**
This index records hardware evidence; it does not constitute a
compliance review, an electrical safety review, or a regulatory
conformity claim. HW-005 and COMPLIANCE-001 are **separate** blockers;
neither subsumes the other; both must clear independently.
