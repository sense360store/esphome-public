`Sense360 Relay` board, revision **R4** (`S360-310-R4`). It exists to
close the documentation gap recorded at
[`docs/hardware/hardware-artifact-policy.md` Follow-up PR sequence #3](hardware-artifact-policy.md#follow-up-pr-sequence)

evidence — a module-side `S360-310-R4` schematic and a curated
artifact index built from it — does not exist in this repository
and was not supplied to the task environment that produced this PR.
This PR therefore records the audit as **pending** and inventories
the exact evidence required by any future PR that wants to close it.
The HW-ASSETS-310 schematic-ingest prerequisite has since landed (see
the addendum above); the reconciliation prerequisites have not.

## Known assumptions

Stated as assumptions, not as facts. None of these is sufficient
to mark `S360-310` `verified` or to mark `fan_relay.yaml`
`confirmed-ok`.

- **Core-side `J4` is the relay connector.** [`s360-100-r4-core.md`
  §J4](s360-100-r4-core.md#j4--relay-module-connector-3-pin)
  captures `J4` as a 3-pin connector for the relay module with nets
  `+5V`, `Relay`, `GND`. This is Core-side evidence only.
- **Module-side relay drive signal originates at ESP32 `IO3`.**
  [`s360-100-r4-core.md` Pin assignments](s360-100-r4-core.md#pin-assignments)
  line 100 places the `Relay` net at `IO3`. The module-side
  schematic must confirm that the cable physically carries this
  signal to the relay coil-drive circuit on the module side.
- **The module side carries a relay, snubber, coil driver, and an
  on-board indicator.** The hardware-catalog description
  (`On/off relay for bathroom fans.`) implies this topology. The
  exact relay part (mechanical relay vs SSR), contact rating,
  coil drive transistor, snubber components, isolation strategy,
  and on-board indicator LED are unknown without the module
  schematic.
- **Drive logic level is +3.3 V CMOS from `IO3`.** The Core
  schematic drives `J4` pin 2 directly from `IO3`. Whether the
  module side requires level shifting (e.g. to drive a 5 V coil
  via a transistor) is unknown.
- **Power rail at `J4` pin 1 is `+5V`.** [`s360-100-r4-core.md` §J4](s360-100-r4-core.md#j4--relay-module-connector-3-pin)
  line 149 records `+5V` at `J4` pin 1. Whether the module-side
  relay coil runs from this `+5V` rail or from a separate boost
  rail is unknown without the module schematic.

These assumptions are not invented pin maps. Anything beyond the
list above — relay coil voltage, contact rating, snubber values,
mains-side topology, harness identity, fault behaviour — is
**unknown** and must come from the module-side schematic.
